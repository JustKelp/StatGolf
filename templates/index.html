      You've Got It!
      That's StatGolf.

      Three holes, one swing at a time. A fresh course drops every morning — come back tomorrow and try to go lower.

      Choose Difficulty

      How to Play

      The Goal
      Each hole has a target yardage. Hit the pin in as few shots as possible by picking real athletes — their stat values become your shot distances.
      Taking a Shot
      Select a club, search for an athlete, pick a season. Their stat value for that club's category becomes your distance — you won't see the number until after you swing.
      Your Clubs
      Each club uses a different stat category. The Driver (career yards, total points) covers big distances. Mid-irons use season stats. The Putter uses precision stats like assists or rings. The Driver is tee-only — used once per hole. All other clubs can be reused.
      Strategy
      Picking the right athlete matters. A player with 5,000 passing yards covers more ground than one with 2,000. Overshoot the pin and you'll need to chip back. Use precision stats to finish close.
      Hazards
      Bunker — next shot distance is halved. Water — take a drop (+1 stroke) or rehit. Hazard yardage ranges are shown on the course.
      Scoring
      Par = expected strokes. Complete all 3 holes for your total. The Rarity score shows how unique your athlete picks were — lower means rarer.
      Gimme
      Get within the gimme range and the hole completes automatically. Amateur: 20 yds · Scratch: 10 yds
